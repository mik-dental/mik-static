Frequently asked questions

What implant systems does ImplaTorque Versa support?
ImplaTorque Versa comes with 16 precision drivers compatible with most major implant systems, making it truly universal for prosthetic restorations.
What is the torque range, and how do I set it?
The wrench is fully adjustable from 10 Ncm to 70 Ncm. Simply rotate the torque dial to your desired value based on manufacturer recommendations.
Can I use this for both tightening and loosening implant components?
Yes. ImplaTorque Versa features bi-directional (clockwise and anti-clockwise) function, ideal for placing and removing screws.
Is this suitable for patients with limited mouth opening or posterior implants?
Absolutely. The slim, low-profile design makes it easy to access hard-to-reach areas, especially in posterior regions or tight arches.
What if I don’t know which implant system was used in a referred case?
That’s where Versa shines. With its universal driver kit, you can confidently restore implants even when the system is unfamiliar.
Is the wrench autoclavable?
Yes, all components are fully autoclavable at 134°C, including the wrench, drivers, and holder.
How durable is the tool?
Made from surgical-grade stainless steel, ImplaTorque Versa is designed for daily use, with high resistance to wear, corrosion, and repeated sterilization.
What if I have an issue with the wrench head?
The kit has you covered as it comes with a spare wrench head.
How do I know which driver fits which implant system?
Each of the 16 drivers is clearly labeled and mapped for compatibility with common implant systems. A driver reference chart is included on the box (back side).
Is it suitable for full-arch or All-on-4 prosthetics?
Yes. Versa is especially useful in multi-unit prosthetic cases, including All-on-4 and full-arch restorations, thanks to its precision torque and broad compatibility.
